6

Publish it so it stays online

This is how your site gets a real web address that stays up for free. Take it slowly, the exact names matter.

1. Make a free GitHub account. Go to github.com, sign up, and pick a username. Your username becomes part of your web address, so choose something you are happy to share. Confirm your email when GitHub asks.

2. Create a new repository. A repository, or repo, is just a folder for your project. Click the plus sign in the top right and choose "New repository." Name it exactly username.github.io, swapping in your real username, all lowercase. That precise name is the trick that turns the repo into a live website. Set it to Public, and click "Create repository."

3. Upload your files. On the new repo page, click the blue text that says "uploading an existing file." Drag in both index.html and showcase.html at the same time. Scroll down and click the green "Commit changes" button to save them.

4. Turn on GitHub Pages. Click the Settings tab at the top of the repo, then Pages in the left sidebar. Under "Branch," open the dropdown, choose main, leave the folder as / (root), and click Save.

5. Visit your live site. Wait about a minute, then go to https://username.github.io with your username. Your site is now live and free to keep. If it does not load right away, give it another minute and refresh, since the first publish can be slow.

6. Test it now that it is live. Click between your two pages to check the nav links work, and try anything interactive on the page. If something is off, fix the files and upload them again the same way.
